Summary of Research paper

In comparing active and passive funds across large-cap, mid-cap, and small-cap categories, distinct trends emerge. Actively managed funds generally boast higher total Assets Under Management (AUM), signaling strong investor confidence in the expertise of fund managers to outperform the market. However, passive funds, particularly in the mid-cap and small-cap segments, exhibit competitive and sometimes superior average 1-year returns. This suggests that while active management may attract larger investments and offer potential for outperformance, passive strategies, especially in certain market segments, demonstrate efficacy in closely tracking or even surpassing market benchmarks, appealing to investors seeking cost-effective and consistent returns.

Ultimately, the choice between active and passive funds hinges on investors' preferences, risk tolerance, and investment objectives. While active funds may provide opportunities for potentially higher returns, they often come with higher management fees and the risk of underperformance. Conversely, passive funds offer simplicity, lower costs, and the ability to closely mirror market performance. Investors should carefully weigh these factors and consider diversifying their portfolios with a blend of active and passive strategies to align with their long-term financial goals and risk appetites.
